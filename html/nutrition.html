Nutrition

Understanding Nutrients

Nutrients are chemical compounds in food that are crucial to human growth, disease prevention, and overall health. They are divided into macros (needed in large amounts) and micros (needed in smaller amounts).

Protein

Macronutrient

Large molecules consisting of amino acids that our bodies need to function properly.

Importance & Function: Essential for building and repairing tissues, preserving muscle mass, and making enzymes and hormones.

Food Sources: Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and nuts.

Carbohydrates

Macronutrient

Sugar molecules that your body breaks down into glucose for energy.

Importance & Function: Your body's primary and preferred source of energy. Fuels brain function and physical activity.

Food Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, beans, and oats.

Fats

Macronutrient

Essential lipids that provide a concentrated source of energy.

Importance & Function: Crucial for nutrient absorption, nerve transmission, protecting organs, and hormone production.

Food Sources: Avocados, olive oil, nuts, seeds, and fatty fish.

Vitamins & Minerals

Micronutrient

Essential dietary elements needed in varying, but small quantities.

Importance & Function: Orchestrate bone health, brain function, immune system boosting, and disease prevention.

Food Sources: A diverse diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole foods.

Water

Essential Nutrient

The most crucial nutrient; you can only survive a few days without it.

Importance & Function: Acts as a solvent and temperature regulator. Flushes waste, lubricates joints, aids in digestion and nutrient absorption.

Food Sources: Drinking water, water-rich fruits and vegetables, and teas.
